Cold War Space Race

It is impossible to talk about early space development without mentioning the Cold war and its consequences. With both USSR and The USA wanting to prove idological and scientific superiority they began the race for space. The two countries were competing to be the first to do things like launch a rocket into space, send a satellite into orbit, and land a person on the moon. It all started in 1957 when the Soviet Union sent the first satellite, Sputnik, into orbit. The United States followed with its own satellite, Explorer 1, in 1958. The race continued for many years, and in 1969, the United States won by landing the first people on the moon with the Apollo 11 mission.
