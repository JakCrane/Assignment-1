Cold War Space Race

The space race was a competition between the United States and the Soviet Union to see who could explore space the most. The two countries were trying to be the first to do things like launch a rocket into space, send a satellite into orbit, and land a person on the moon. They both wanted to show the world that their country was the best at science and technology. It was like a big game where each side tried to outdo the other. It all started in 1957 when the Soviet Union sent the first satellite, Sputnik, into orbit. The United States followed with its own satellite, Explorer 1, in 1958. The race continued for many years, and in 1969, the United States won by landing the first people on the moon with the Apollo 11 mission.
